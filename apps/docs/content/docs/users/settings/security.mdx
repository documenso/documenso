## Change Your Password

If you signed up with email and password (not Google or another provider), you can change your password at any time.

{/* prettier-ignore */}
<Steps>
  <Step>
    Click your avatar in the top right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Go to **Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Open the **Security** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Enter your current password, new password, and confirmation, then click **Update password**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
  The password change form only appears if you have a password-based account. If you signed up with
  Google or another provider, you won't see this option.
</Callout>

## Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication (2FA) and passkeys are used for high-security and high-compliance signatures in addition to account login.

Two-factor authentication adds a second layer of security by requiring a code from an authenticator app when you sign in or sign documents.

![Enable two-factor authentication](/get-started-images/documenso-enable-2-factor-authentication.webp)

### Enable 2FA

{/* prettier-ignore */}
<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Open 2FA setup

    Go to **Settings**, open the **Security** tab, find the Two factor authentication section, and click **Enable 2FA**.

  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Scan the QR code

    Open your authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, 1Password, etc.) and scan the QR code. If you can't scan, reveal the setup key and enter it manually.

  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Verify and save

    Enter the 6-digit code from your app, click **Enable**, then save your recovery codes (see [Recovery Codes](#recovery-codes) below).

  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
  Logging in with Google does not require a 2FA code. As an authentication provider, your Google
  account is considered secure (e.g. you can configure 2FA on your Google account separately).
</Callout>

<Callout type="info">
  Logging in with a passkey does not require a 2FA code. Passkeys are considered two-factor by
  design â€” the passkey itself is the first factor, and access to the device that holds it is the
  second factor.
</Callout>

### Disable 2FA

{/* prettier-ignore */}
<Steps>
  <Step>
    Go to **Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Open the **Security** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Disable 2FA**, enter a code from your authenticator app, then click **Disable**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Recovery Codes

Recovery codes let you access your account if you lose access to your authenticator app. When you enable 2FA, Documenso generates a set of backup codes.

### View Recovery Codes

{/* prettier-ignore */}
<Steps>
  <Step>
    Go to **Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Open the **Security** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **View Codes** in the Recovery codes section.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Enter a code from your authenticator app to view your recovery codes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Store Recovery Codes Safely

- Click **Download** to save them as a text file
- Store them in a secure location (password manager, safe, etc.)
- Each code can only be used once

<Callout type="warn">
  If you lose both your authenticator app and your recovery codes, you will be locked out of your
  account. Store your recovery codes securely.
</Callout>

## Passkeys

Passkeys provide passwordless authentication using biometrics, hardware security keys, or password managers. You can use passkeys to sign in to Documenso and to authenticate when signing documents.

![Passkeys management page](/get-started-images/documenso-passkeys-page.webp)

### Add a Passkey

{/* prettier-ignore */}
<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Open passkeys

    Go to **Settings**, open the **Security** tab, then click **Manage passkeys**.

  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Add and name your passkey

    Click **Add passkey**, enter a name (e.g. "MacBook Touch ID", "YubiKey"), then follow your browser's prompts to complete registration.

  </Step>
</Steps>

![Add passkey dialog](/get-started-images/documenso-add-passkey-box.webp)

### Manage Passkeys

From the passkeys page, you can:

- **Rename** a passkey by clicking the edit button
- **Delete** a passkey by clicking the delete button

<Callout type="info">
  Passkeys are device-specific. If you use multiple devices, add a passkey on each one.
</Callout>

## Active Sessions

View and manage all devices currently logged into your account.

### View Sessions

Go to **Settings**, open the **Security** tab, then click **Manage sessions**.

The sessions table shows events:

| Column      | Description                    |
| ----------- | ------------------------------ |
| Device      | Browser and operating system   |
| IP Address  | The IP address of the session  |
| Last Active | When the session was last used |
| Created     | When the session started       |

Your current session is marked with a "Current" badge.

### Revoke a Session

<Tabs items={['Revoke one session', 'Log out all']}>
  <Tab value="Revoke one session">Find the session in the list and click **Revoke**.</Tab>
  <Tab value="Log out all">
    Click **Log out all** at the top of the page and confirm. Your current session stays active; all
    others are terminated.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Security Activity

Monitor recent security-related events on your account. Go to **Settings**, open the **Security** tab, then click **View activity**.

The activity log shows the events that have occurred on your account. Examples include:

| Event            | Description                           |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Sign-in attempts | Login attempts to your account        |
| Password changes | When your password was changed        |
| 2FA changes      | When 2FA was enabled or disabled      |
| Session changes  | When sessions were created or revoked |

## Linked Accounts

View and manage login methods connected to your account (Google, email/password, etc.). Go to **Settings**, open the **Security** tab, and click **Manage linked accounts**.

To remove a login method:

{/* prettier-ignore */}
<Steps>
  <Step>
    Go to **Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Open the **Security** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Manage linked accounts**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Find the provider in the list, click **Unlink**, and confirm.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warn">
  Ensure you have at least one login method available before unlinking an account.
</Callout>

## Delete Your Account

Account deletion permanently removes all your data, including documents, signatures, and settings. This action cannot be undone.

### Before Deleting

- Download any documents you need to keep
- Cancel any active subscriptions
- Disable 2FA (required before deletion)

### Delete Your Account

{/* prettier-ignore */}
<Steps>
  <Step>
    Go to **Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Open the **Profile** tab and scroll to the **Delete Account** section.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Delete Account**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Type your email address to confirm and click **Confirm Deletion**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="error">
  Account deletion is permanent and irreversible. All documents, signatures, and account data will
  be permanently deleted.
</Callout>

## Security Best Practices

<Accordions type="multiple">
  <Accordion title="Use a strong, unique password">
    Avoid reusing passwords from other services.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Enable two-factor authentication">
    2FA significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Store recovery codes securely">
    Keep them in a password manager or other secure location.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Add passkeys on trusted devices">
    Passkeys provide phishing-resistant authentication.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Review active sessions regularly">
    Revoke any sessions you don't recognize.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Monitor security activity">Check for unexpected sign-ins or changes.</Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use separate API tokens per integration">
    If one is compromised, revoke only that token.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Set expiration dates on API tokens">
    Shorter lifespans reduce risk if a token is leaked.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
